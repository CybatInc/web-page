User Guide_

The Intelligence Dashboard is your real-time command center for autonomous threat monitoring. This guide covers every panel, how the engine processes threats, and how enforcement actions are applied.

Open Dashboard →

1. Dashboard Layout

After logging in at intelligence.cybat.co, you land on the main Intelligence Dashboard. The left sidebar gives you access to all sections based on your role.

Metric Cards

Five cards at the top of the dashboard show your organisation's security posture at a glance: Data Ingested, Threats Blocked, High Severity, Medium Severity, and Low Severity. All figures reflect the selected time period (Day / Month / Year).

3D Threat Globe

A rotating Earth visualization showing the geographic origin of all detected threats. Red points represent origins with high-severity incidents; amber points represent medium-severity. Click any point to open a breakdown by severity, then click View incidents → to filter the Investigations feed to that location.

[ screenshot-globe.png ]

Investigations Feed

A live list of all detected threats sorted by recency. Each row shows source IP, threat level, target URL, timestamp, and enforcement verdict. Click any row to open the full incident detail panel. Use the 👍 / 👎 buttons to provide feedback — thumbs down on a blocked IP opens an unblock confirmation dialog.

[ screenshot-incidents.png ]

Incident Detail Panel

Opens over the feed. Shows the full Gemini AI analysis narrative, all log evidence records that triggered the detection, the enforcement decision and status, and the Slack notification dispatched to your team. Admins can unblock an IP directly from this panel using the 👎 feedback button.

2. How the Engine Processes a Threat

Each log record moves through the following pipeline automatically. The entire sequence completes in under 5 seconds from log emission to enforcement.

01
Ingestion — Cloud Logging routes your project's logs to a dedicated Pub/Sub topic. The Cybat ingestion layer (Fluent Bit + pubsub-bridge running on GKE) pulls messages continuously and forwards them to the SOC agent.
02
IP Grouping — Logs from the same batch are grouped by source IP to build a per-IP request picture before analysis.
03
Subscription Check — The engine validates your organization's active Marketplace subscription via GCP Service Control before proceeding.
04
Whitelist Filter — IPs present in your organisation's whitelist are removed from the batch entirely. No analysis, no incident record, no cost. Traffic from trusted IPs is completely ignored by the engine.
05
Verdict Cache — IPs seen in the last 24 hours skip Gemini re-analysis and are enforced immediately using the cached verdict, eliminating redundant AI calls.
06
Gemini Analysis — New IPs are analyzed by Vertex AI Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite. The model receives the full log context and returns a structured verdict: threat level (high / medium / low / none) and a human-readable explanation.
07
Enforcement — For high and medium verdicts, the engine attempts VPC Firewall deny first (Layer 3/4). If the VPC network is not discoverable from the logs, it falls back to Cloud Armor IP block (Layer 7). Enforcement is targeted to the specific GCP project the logs originated from.
08
Persistence & Alerting — The incident, AI analysis, and evidence records are written to the dashboard. A Slack notification is dispatched to your configured webhook with the full threat summary and enforcement outcome.

3. Threat Levels & Enforcement Behavior

Level Action Dashboard Color
HIGH Immediate block — VPC Firewall deny or Cloud Armor IP rule. Slack alert dispatched. red
MEDIUM Block applied. Slack alert dispatched. Incident flagged for review. amber
LOW No enforcement. Verdict logged and cached for 24 hours. muted white
NONE Clean traffic. No action. Verdict cached for 24 hours to skip future analysis. not shown

4. Feedback & Manual Unblock

Every incident in the feed and detail panel has 👍 / 👎 feedback buttons. Feedback trains the system's false-positive rate reporting and helps your team audit enforcement decisions.

Thumbs Up — Correct Classification

Marks the incident as a true positive. The feedback is recorded and counted in your monthly signal-to-noise report.

Thumbs Down on a Detected (not blocked) Incident

Marks the incident as a false positive. The feedback is recorded immediately.

Thumbs Down on a Blocked Incident — Admin Only

A confirmation dialog appears asking whether to remove the active block. Clicking Yes, unblock immediately removes the Cloud Armor or VPC Firewall rule for that IP and marks the incident expired. The feedback is also saved as a false positive. Clicking No, keep blocked saves only the feedback without touching the enforcement rule.

Note: unblocking is scoped to your organisation only and requires the Admin role. The action is logged with the email of the admin who performed it.

5. IP Whitelist

The Whitelist menu item (visible to admins only) lets you register trusted IP addresses that the engine should never analyze or block — for example, your office network, a monitoring service, or a known partner API.

How it works

  • Whitelisted IPs are filtered out of every log batch before the AI analysis step — no Vertex AI call is made, no incident is created, and no usage is counted for those requests.
  • Whitelisted IPs are also exempt from enforcement: even if a cached verdict exists for the IP, no block will be applied.
  • The whitelist is scoped to your organisation. Each org manages its own list independently.

Managing the whitelist

  • Navigate to Whitelist in the left sidebar (admin role required).
  • Enter an IPv4 or IPv6 address and an optional note explaining why it is trusted, then click Add IP.
  • To remove an entry, click Remove in the corresponding row. The change takes effect on the next log batch.

Tip: if an IP is currently blocked and you also want to whitelist it, unblock it first using the 👎 feedback dialog, then add it to the whitelist to prevent future blocks.

6. Slack Alert Configuration

Slack alerts are configured per organization during onboarding. Each alert includes:

  • Source IP and geographic location
  • Threat level and the Gemini model's explanation
  • Enforcement action taken (firewall rule name or Cloud Armor rule)
  • GCP project affected
  • Timestamp and incident ID for cross-referencing the dashboard

To update your Slack webhook URL, contact support or use the settings panel inside the Intelligence Portal.
